Kaitlin Huben, for Dance in NYC
  • In its purest form, dance is an art.
  • After hundreds or thousands of hours of work, the choreographer and dancers present their ephemeral art for the audience to interpret.
  • The curtain rises, the dancers dance; the curtain falls, the attendees clap.
  • What the audience members rarely ponder, however, is the work that went into the performance: the potentially unartful and stressful auditions, rehearsals, and meetings that brought the art to life.
